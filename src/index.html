  • From 2016-2020 in Metro Vancouver, 143 pedestrians were killed and thousands more injured in traffic collisions, according ICBC data.   • 104 Ave. and King George Blvd.

    Collisions at this busy intersection have fallen dramatically since 2016, when 8 pedestrians were injured or killed by vehicles.

  • South Fraser Way and Trethewey

    Abbotsford had the third highest number of pedestrian collisions after Vancouver and Surrey – despite having one-third the population. Most collisions occur along South Fraser Way.

  • Boundary and Kingway

    From 2016-2020, 24 people were injured or killed in collisions at this intersection. In 2018 alone, ten people were struck by vehicles – the highest single-year count in the study period.

  • No. 3 Road and Granville Ave.

    Each year in Richmond, roughly 20 per cent of pedestrian injuries take place along No. 3 Road. The intersection at Granville Ave. is the most dangerous – at least 16 people have been injured or killed there since 2016.
